## **Stewart Brand & Larry Brilliant: The WELL (1985)**

### **What problem existed?**

By the mid-80s, the "Internet" was still mostly the playground of the military and academia. Regular people had no way to talk to each other through their computers unless they were in the same room. There was no "Social Media," no "Forums," and no "Comments Section." We had the connectivity (TCP/IP), but we didn't have a **digital town square.**

### **What did this person actually do?**

Stewart Brand and Larry Brilliant launched **The WELL** in Sausalito, California. It was a Bulletin Board System (BBS) that people accessed via dial-up modems. Unlike other technical boards, The WELL was built on the philosophy of **"You Own Your Own Words" (YOYOW).** It attracted writers, hackers, artists, and Deadheads, turning a computer network into a dense, text-based conversation that lasted for years.

### **Why did it unlock something?**

It proved that **Online Community** was possible. It was the first "Social Network" in the modern sense. It moved the "click" from "How do I move this data?" to "Who am I talking to?" It established the etiquette, the flame wars, and the deep communal support that would define the next 40 years of the internet. If you want to understand why humans are so addicted to social AI today, the answer started in the text-only threads of The WELL.
