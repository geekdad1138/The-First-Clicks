## **Herman Hollerith: The Tabulating Machine (1890)**

### **What problem existed?**

The U.S. Census Bureau had a crisis. The 1880 census took eight years to process by hand. With the population exploding, they estimated the 1890 census would take over a decade, meaning the data would be obsolete before it was even finished. The government was drowning in its own data.

### **What did this person actually do?**

Hollerith invented an **electromechanical tabulating machine**. He captured individual citizen data by punching holes into cards (similar to Babbage's idea, but for data storage rather than instructions). His machine used electrical feelers to "read" these holes and increment mechanical counters.

### **Why did it unlock something?**

It was the birth of **automated data processing**. Hollerith proved that data could be "machine-readable." By using his system, the 1890 census was completed in just **two years** instead of ten. He turned information into a tangible, stackable, and searchable resource.
